About

I study problems on data management and data science. My research is driven by the need to build trustworthy and responsible decision-making systems. Recently, I have been developing systems that facilitate explainability, fairness and accountability of data-driven decision-making systems.

Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute at the University of California San Diego where I was involved in building Lewis, a system that uses probabilistic contrastive counterfactuals to explain the decisions of black-box algorithms. Before that, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University where I researched database management for causal inference, and taught the undergraduate machine learning course.

I earned my Ph.D. from the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University where my dissertation focused on resolving conflicts in data integrated from disparate data sources. In a previous life, I graduated with M.S. and B.S. in Mathematics and Computing from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, India.

I am actively looking for prospective students (Ph.D., Master’s, and undergraduate) and postdoctoral researchers to join my research group. Email me if you are interested.
