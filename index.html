About

I study problems on data management and data science. My research is driven by the need to build trustworthy and responsible decision-making systems.

Recently, I have been working in the area of responsible data science, developing systems aimed at facilitating explainability, fairness, and accountability of data-driven decision-making systems. I have also worked on problems in data integration, focusing on resolving conflicts in data integrated from disparate data sources and ensuring end-users can trust the quality of the integrated data.

Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of California San Diego and a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University. I earned my Ph.D. from Purdue Computer Science and graduated with M.S. and B.S. in Mathematics and Computing from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, India.
